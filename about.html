Influences in Architecture

This website is created to address the influences of China, India, and the west on Southeast Asia. While it's true that each polity has their own cultural identity, it is these 3 influences that unite this region in culture, religion, and most visibly in their art.

Southeast Asia was under Indian sphere of cultural influence starting around 290 BC until around the 15th century, when Hindu-Buddhist influence was absorbed by local politics. During the same time, Southeast Asia is also under the protectorate of China. With some nations paying direct tribute to China, and others only engaging in trade with China. Most Notable example being Zheng He collecting tributes throughout Asia and Africa.

However over time, India and China both began to lose influence over Southeast Asia. With China having a more seclusion policy and India losing power due to infighting, it left Southeast Asia open to imperialism. First as economic partners, then exploited and colonized by various European nations. Even though each country has gained independence from their colonizers, they have forever been affected by each sphere of influence. The goal of this website is to see what they've left behind.

As these three influences grew, the way they disseminated in Southeast Asia resembled a mandala, often overlapping and always having the strongest concentration in the center. Each of the 11 nations in Southeast Asia started to develop their own cultural identity based on how close they are to the center of each mandala. This website uses six different examples, two of each influence, where I show clear distinctions between each influence as well as how it mixed with the underlying culture.

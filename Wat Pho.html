Wat Pho in Bangkok, Thailand

  1. Home
  2. Sinification

Posted on April 14th, 2021 at 5:00 PM

Bangkok is the capital of the Kingdom of Thailand. Bangkok itself means the city of the gods. And Bangkok has a heart in the inner Rattanakosin city, which is the first area of Wat Pho that was constructed. This area is called "Phra Nakhon" and it is an area that has accumulated Thai cultural heritage and wisdom for the future generations to see, learn, get to know, to be proud of and to bring all knowledge to follow. Wat Pho or the official name that Temple of the Reclining Buddha Is a royal temple of the first class and is the temple of the reign of King Rama IX of the Chakri Dynasty. And at the base of Chukchi enshrines the Buddha Deva Sculpture the main Buddha image in the Ubosot (a place that contains the Buddha image of His Highness as well).

Wat Pho is one of the largest and oldest wats in Bangkok covering an area of 50 rai or 80,000 square metres. It is home to more than one thousand Buddha images, as well as one of the largest single Buddha images at 46 metres (151 ft) in length. The Wat Pho complex consists of two walled compounds bisected by Chetuphon Road running in the east–west direction. The larger northern walled compound, the phutthawat, is open to visitors and contains the finest buildings dedicated to the Buddha, including the bot with its four directional viharn, and the temple housing the reclining Buddha. The southern compound, the sankhawat, contains the residential quarters of the monks and a school. The perimeter wall of the main temple complex has sixteen gates, two of which serve as entrances for the public.

Wat Pho has clear Chinese influence due to the site plans of the complex. Like the Forbidden palace, there is an inner temple that only specific people can go to, and like other Chinese architecture, this site contains wood bracketing and roofing similar to other Chinese buildings.

It should be noted that this temple is a Theravada Buddhist temple with many symbols similar to Indian temples like in Kailasnath. Some images in this temple that are similar is the reclining Buddha similar to Buddha Parinirvana in Ajanta. However, while this temple does show many Indian motifs, it is displayed with Chinese architecture and influence. Unlike Indian temples, they spared no expense in showing the wealth of the country like the wealth displayed in the Forbidden City.

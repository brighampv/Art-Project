Influences of Southeast Asia

Indianization

Being under cultural or political influence of India. Most of Southeast Asia has been influenced by India primarily by trade. However, while this influence is not seen in contemporary life, it has greatly affected their art, religion, and language.

Learn More at Wikipedia

Sinification

This is the process by which non-Chinese societies come under the influence of Chinese culture. Whether in direct or indirect control, the extent to which China exerted its power to regional polities determined how much Chinese culture were exported to Southeast Asia.

Learn More at Wikipedia

Westernization

Most of Southeast Asia's polities had been created by conquest and coercion. European rule was not welcome. But Southeast Asia's reaction especially how they compromised and negotiated, determined who they are today.

Learn More at Wikipedia

Featured Architecture

Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat is an enormous Buddhist temple complex located in northern Cambodia. Originally dedicated to the Hindu god Vishnu, Angkor Wat became a Buddhist temple by the end of the 12th century.

Wat Pho

Wat Pho is one of the largest and oldest wats in Bangkok covering an area of 50 rai or 80,000 square metres.

Raffles Hotel

Raffles Hotel is a colonial-style hotel in Singapore. The hotel was named after British statesman Sir Thomas Stamford Raffles, the founder of colonial-era Singapore.

Pura Basekih

Besakih Temple is a pura complex in the village of Besakih on the slopes of Mount Agung in eastern Bali, Indonesia.

Imperial City of Hue

The Imperial City Hue. The Hue Imperial City was one of the most important political centres of the country of Vietnam.

Malacañang Palace

Malacañang Palace is the official residence and principal workplace of the President of the Philippines.
